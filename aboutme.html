250 Words About Me

I am a second year computer science major at University of California, Berkeley. That being said, I am not focused just on computer science. I live by a simple philosophy, Never Stop Learning. This philosophy guides how I learn, lead, and have fun.  I immerse myself in the subjects that I pursue, such as computer science. I surround myself with material, not just course material, but also projects and various readings on the applications of the subjects I am studying. If I am not coding or learning a programming concept, I will often be reading some science, economic, or philosophy article. These seemingly unrelated subjects are all ones that I find interesting and useful, and I attempt to integrate the knowledge from them into my life. into my life through coursework or hobbies.

When not expanding my knowledge, I can often be found working out or learning a new skill such as sewing (badly). During this time,I learn patience and how to push my limits. I then bring these traits to my projects and work.   By living a well rounded life, I have realized that things are more connected than they seem. I see patterns emerge between fields that I once thought were very different. The connections that I see have prompted me to have an interdisciplinary mindset- one that focuses on seeing how I can apply knowledge from certain fields to different ones. This mindset is one that allows me to think of creative solutions to difficult problems.
